What is PMDD?

Premenstrual Dysphoric Disorder (PMDD) is a more severe form of Premenstrual Syndrome (PMS) that can cause many side effects. The cause of PMDD is unknown. They believe it to be an abnormal reaction to hormonal changes during the menstrual cycle. More information about PMDD can be found at hopkinsmedicine.org.

Symptoms

What are the symptoms for PMDD?

PMDD can show up differently in every woman. There are numerous symptoms, usually showing up within a week before or after your menstrual cycle. Some of these symptoms include:

  • Irritability
  • Anger
  • Depression
  • Anxiety
  • Lack of control
  • Nausea
  • Acne
  • Headaches

Treatments for PMDD

There are many treatments for PMDD, including:

  • Exercise
  • Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRI)
  • Birth control pills
  • Changes in diet
  • Stress management
